Using only the selected sources, give me [Concept Name] as a short, plain-English story that preserves the chain of purposes.

Important constraint:
I do not want a static summary.
I do not want a textbook paraphrase.
I want a short, cohesive story that lets me mentally see:
- what someone or something is trying to achieve
- what obstacle or limitation exists
- how this concept becomes the way through that obstacle
- what leverage, mechanism, or pattern makes it work
- what intermediate effect it creates
- what final outcome or stake it serves

Structure the answer exactly like this:

1. Big-picture story
· In 4–6 sentences, explain [Concept Name] as one connected story in plain English.
· The story should naturally preserve this chain:
  - starting goal or need
  - obstacle, limitation, or problem
  - the role this concept plays
  - how it works at a high level
  - what it causes next
  - what final outcome or payoff it leads to
· Do not present these as bullet fragments.
· Make it feel like one flowing explanation that a learner can visualize.

2. Bottom-line essence
· In 1 sentence, complete this:
  “Basically, this whole thing is about ______.”

Requirements:
· Preserve the chain of purposes. Do not flatten the answer into a static description.
· Write like a strong teacher helping the learner see the whole arc at once.
· Prefer ordinary, visual, cause-and-effect language.
· If the concept is not actor-driven, still explain it as a chain from starting condition to final significance.
· Do not over-focus on technical details.
· Do not strip away the final stake or the reason the concept matters.
· The goal is to leave the learner thinking:
  “Now I can see the whole thing happening from beginning to end.”
