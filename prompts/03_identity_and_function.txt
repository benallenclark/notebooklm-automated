Using only the selected sources, teach me what [Concept Name] is now that I already understand:
- why it matters
- what larger purpose it serves
- and what this whole thing is really about at a big-picture level

Important constraint:
At this point, I do NOT need another broad purpose overview.
I already have the story and the reason it matters.
Now I want the concept to become mechanically understandable, structurally clear, and technically exact.

This prompt should build naturally on the earlier prompts:
- first, make the concept feel concrete and graspable
- then explain how it works
- then lock in the exact meaning
- then prevent the most likely confusion

Structure the answer exactly like this:

1. Whole idea in one simple sentence
· Give the clearest plain-English statement of what the concept is.

2. How it works at a high level
· In a few sentences, explain how the concept actually works.
· Focus on the main causal flow or mechanism, not every detail.

3. Why this mechanism works
· Explain what basic condition, weakness, structure, rule, pattern, or leverage makes this concept able to work at all.

4. First-principles breakdown
· What the concept is fundamentally about
· Its essential parts, stages, or ingredients, explained in beginner-friendly language

5. Technical definition
· Give the more formal, precise, and technically accurate definition.

6. One common mix-up to avoid
· Name the most likely thing a learner could confuse this concept with.
· Then explain the sharpest boundary between them in a few sentences.

7. One compact category lock
· In one short phrase, say what kind of thing this concept is, but only at the level needed to stabilize understanding.
· Do not turn this into a long classification exercise.

Requirements:
· Assume the learner already knows the purpose chain and big-picture story.
· Do not repeat the broader “why does this matter?” explanation unless absolutely necessary.
· Start with understandability, then move into exactness.
· Section 1 should make the concept feel easy to mentally grab.
· Section 2 should explain the main working in a way the learner can visualize.
· Section 3 should answer the natural question:
  “Why is this even able to work?”
· Section 4 should simplify the concept into its essential moving parts without becoming vague.
· Section 5 should be the most precise part.
· Section 6 should reduce confusion, not launch into a full comparison essay.
· Section 7 should only stabilize the concept’s identity; do not over-focus on taxonomy.
· Do not drift back into broad purpose summaries, final stakes, or actor motives unless needed to clarify the mechanism.
· The goal is to leave me feeling:
  “Now I know what this thing is, how it works, why that working is possible, what its essential structure is, and what I should not confuse it with.”
