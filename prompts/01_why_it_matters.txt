Using only the selected sources, explain the purpose of [Concept Name] by following its chain of whys from the surface level to the deepest grounded end that the sources support.

Important constraint:
Do not stop at the first purpose.
Do not stop at the technical mechanism.
Do not stop at the system-level outcome if a deeper human, strategic, practical, or domain-level reason is clearly implied.

My mind naturally keeps asking:
“Why?”
“For what?”
“And what deeper goal does that serve?”
So your job is to follow the purpose chain until the concept feels grounded in the most concrete final stake the sources can support.

Structure the answer exactly like this:

1. Surface purpose
· In one plain sentence, say the most immediate purpose of the concept.

2. Why that purpose matters
· Explain what problem, gap, task, question, or missing ability makes that immediate purpose necessary.

3. Deeper objective underneath it
· Explain what larger goal, state, outcome, or end is being served underneath the surface purpose.

4. Ultimate technical outcome
· State the final technical, operational, structural, or system-level effect this chain is trying to produce.

5. Final stake / actor motive
· Explain what achieving that ultimate technical outcome most likely lets the actor, user, system, or decision-maker actually gain, accomplish, avoid, influence, control, obtain, protect, or change.

6. Full purpose ladder in one line
· Complete this sentence:
“[Concept Name] matters because it helps with ______, which serves ______, which produces ______, so that ultimately ______.”

Requirements:
· Follow the purpose chain until it reaches the most concrete final stake supported by the sources.
· If the concept is mainly descriptive, trace the chain in terms of what it lets someone notice, explain, predict, distinguish, or reason about, and why that matters.
· If the concept is mainly operational, trace the chain in terms of what it enables, what that produces, and what final outcome that serves.
· If the concept is mainly formal, trace the chain in terms of what it lets someone prove, model, infer, measure, or optimize, and why that matters.
· Section 4 and section 5 must be different:
  - Section 4 = the final technical or system-level result
  - Section 5 = the larger practical, strategic, human, or domain-level reason that result is valuable
· If the sources do not explicitly state the final stake, give the strongest source-grounded likely motive and clearly mark it as an inference.
· Do not invent motives that are not supported or strongly implied.
· The goal is to make me feel:
“Now I know not just what this concept does, but what it is ultimately in service of.”
